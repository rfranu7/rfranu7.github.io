Lesson 02: Design Principles

Contrast

Lemonade Insurance Company

View Now

This is a screenshot of Lemonade Insurance's homepage

You can immediately notice the call to action button from Lemonade Insurance. Their use of pink against a black and white background makes the button and the link below it standout. This makes it easy for the user to decide. Their clean and minimal use of black font with the white background makes the message easy to read and understand.

Visual Hierarchy

Evernote Corporation

View Now

This is a screenshot of Evernote's homepage.

Evernote has good visual hierarchy. They have utilized the F-shaped reading pattern. The main headline "Your notes. Organized. Effortless." stands out by its scale and helps the reader to understand how the app can help them. They have also included a sign-up button with good contrast. Doing so helps the user to have decide to act by clicking the button. Lastly, they have added a snippet of a picture below which makes the user curious enough to scroll down if they are still not convinced with the message that they have just read.

Repetition

Grab Philippines

View Now

This is a screenshot of Grab's homepage

Grab's branding is shown throughout their whole website. Their use of their green brand color, fonts, and other visuals such as in the picture above helped create a good experience for the user. It is easy to figure out what they are selling. Through repeating the designs of each picture, they were able to closely connect each service even if they are different in certain ways.
